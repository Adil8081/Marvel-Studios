Marvel Studios movies in order of release with a brief description of each release :

1. Iron Man (2008) : The film that kicked off the Marvel Cinematic Universe (MCU), Iron Man introduces Tony Stark, a genius billionaire who builds a high-tech suit of armor after being captured by terrorists. His journey from a weapons manufacturer to a superhero is the foundation of the MCU.

2. The Incredible Hulk (2008) : Bruce Banner, a scientist on the run, struggles with his transformation into the Hulk while facing a powerful new enemy, the Abomination. This film expands the MCU with its introduction of the Hulk character.

3. Iron Man 2 (2010) : Tony Stark faces challenges on both personal and professional fronts, with his identity as Iron Man revealed to the world. He confronts Ivan Vanko (Whiplash), who threatens his legacy, while also dealing with the government wanting to seize his technology.

4. Thor (2011) : Thor, the Norse God of Thunder, is banished to Earth after defying his father Odin. He learns humility and teamwork while facing his brother Loki’s betrayal. The introduction of Asgard and its cosmic elements expands the MCU into the mystical realm.

5. Captain America: The First Avenger (2011) : Set during World War II, Steve Rogers becomes the super-soldier Captain America to fight Hydra. The film introduces the first Infinity Stone, the Tesseract, and lays the groundwork for the Avengers initiative.

6. The Avengers (2012) : The Avengers assemble for the first time to stop Loki, who is using an alien army to conquer Earth. This movie brings together Iron Man, Thor, Captain America, Hulk, Black Widow, and Hawkeye as they fight as a team to protect the world.

7. Iron Man 3 (2013) : Following the events of The Avengers, Tony Stark deals with post-traumatic stress and faces the threat of the Mandarin, a mysterious figure with devastating powers. The film explores Tony’s identity without his suit and his journey to reclaim his life.

8. Thor: The Dark World (2013) : Thor teams up with Loki to stop the Dark Elves from using the Aether (one of the Infinity Stones) to destroy the universe. The film further explores Asgard’s mythology and the relationship between Thor and Loki.

9. Captain America: The Winter Soldier (2014) : Captain America discovers a conspiracy within S.H.I.E.L.D. while facing the return of his old friend, Bucky Barnes, who is now the brainwashed Winter Soldier. This movie explores the theme of trust and government control, with significant implications for the MCU’s future.

10. Guardians of the Galaxy (2014) : A group of unlikely heroes, including Peter Quill (Star-Lord), Gamora, Drax, Rocket, and Groot, come together to stop the villain Ronan the Accuser from using a powerful artifact, the Orb (another Infinity Stone), to destroy the universe.
